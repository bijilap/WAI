1. About UIDMS

1.1 What is UIDMS, Unique ID management System?
           •UIDMS is Unique ID management System, is a system that would provide every citizen above 18 years of age, a unique identification card/number, that can be used as a by person as his/her proof of identity acceptable anywhere in India.

1.2. What are the problems that UIDMS seeks to address?
           •A universally acceptable proof of identity is a challenge in India,the singular problem that the UIDMS seeks to solve is that of establishing identity by providing every individual(above 18yrs) a unique identification number (called WAI).Once a person has WAI card their basic identity, including the biometrics is established and hence multiple identity of person can not be established.

1.3. What will UIDMS do?
           •Issue a unique identity number (WAI) to a resident based on basic demographic and biometric information of the person.
           •Authenticate an Indian citizens identity based on his unique ID (i.e. WAI card).

2. WAI Features and Eligibility

2.1. What is WAI?
           •WAI is a term/name to given to the unique ID number provided by UIDMS. No individual can have duplicate WAI card since it is linked to individuals biometric details also. Hence government can use this to identify citizens.

2.2. What are the features and benefits of WAI?
           •One WAI: A beneficiary is, WAI is a unique number, and no resident can have a duplicate number since it is linked to their individual biometrics; thereby identifying fake and ghost identities and hence identification of person becomes easy.

2.3. What is the eligibility criterion to apply for obtaining WAI?
           •The person can apply for WAI, if he/she is a citizen of India, and is above the age of 18 years.

3. Enrollment Process

3.1. What is the enrollment process that will be followed to issue WAI?
           •A resident will have to go to an enrolling agency along with Proof of photo Identity, Proof of address documents, fill up an application form and provide the supporting documentation. The authorized person will verify the information and the documents produced. The demographic data will then be entered in the software and then biometrics information of ten finger prints, and facial photograph of the resident will also be captured and the data will be sent to UIDMS database. (In case of physically challenged person, who cant give their fingerprints, only his photograph will be taken.)

3.2. What information is required to be provided by an individual to get an WAI number?
           •The information that needs to be provided to get WAI is:

•Demographic information required:
a)Name
b)Date of Birth
c)Gender
d)Address
e)Parent/Guardian details
f)Contact details phone and email (optional)

•Biometric Information required:
a)Photo
b)10 finger prints

3.4. Where multiple address proofs are available for a resident (ex. present and native), which proof would UID accept, and where will it send the WAI Letter?
           •The UIDMS asks the resident to confirm the address (from the multiple address proof) on which he wants to receive the WAI letter.

4. Grievances Redressal Mechanism

4.1. Will there be a grievance redressal mechanism?
           •Yes.

4.2. What if a resident gets rejected by UIDMS and is not issued WAI number?
           •The reason for rejection and the steps to be taken post rejection will be communicated to resident using the address/contact details provided by him.

4.3. What if a resident forgets his WAI number/misplaces his WAI letter?
           •The resident can contact us and make a request for sending a second letter communicating the WAI number.

4.4. What can the resident do if there is a spelling mistake/other information error in his WAI letter?
           •During enrolment, when the data is entered, the resident can see the data entry and is expected to point out errors at this stage. In the event that this opportunity is missed, the information can be changed by visiting the enrollment centre along with the various documents.

5. Protection of individual information in WAI system.

5.1. Who will have access to UIDMS database?
           •Residents who have WAI number will be entitled to access their own information stored in the UID database. (But not the information about others)
           •The database itself will be secured against hacking and other forms of cyber attacks.

5.2. Will the residents be allowed to access their own information and make corrections if necessary?
           •A resident will be able to view their complete information in the WAI database, however the person can change his details by only requesting the administrator change of details, along with relevant documents.

6. Differently-Abled

6.1. How will the biometrics of the differently-abled people and people with rugged hands or with no fingers be captured?
           •The UIDMS will take into consideration these exceptions and it will be ensured that these groups are not excluded. In the case of people without hands/ fingers only photo will be used for identity determination and there will be certain markers to determine uniqueness.
